## Source Hierarchy

Every claim in a Psychage article must be traceable to a credible source. We use a 5-tier system — higher tiers carry more weight.

### Tier 1 — Peer-Reviewed Research (Required: ≥40% of citations)

Primary scientific literature with rigorous peer review.

| Source Type | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| Peer-Reviewed Journal | JAMA Psychiatry, The Lancet Psychiatry, American Journal of Psychiatry, Molecular Psychiatry, World Psychiatry, Journal of Abnormal Psychology, Journal of Clinical Psychology |
| Meta-Analysis | Quantitative synthesis of multiple studies |
| Systematic Review | Structured review following PRISMA or equivalent protocol |

### Tier 2 — Government / Institutional

| Source Type | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| NIMH | National Institute of Mental Health publications, fact sheets |
| NIH | National Institutes of Health reports, PubMed-indexed content |
| WHO | World Health Organization guidelines, ICD classification |
| CDC | Centers for Disease Control mental health data |
| SAMHSA | Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration |

**Rule**: Tier 1 + Tier 2 combined must be ≥60% of all citations.

### Tier 3 — Professional Organizations

| Source Type | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| APA (Psychiatric) | American Psychiatric Association practice guidelines |
| APA (Psychological) | American Psychological Association research, style guides |
| NAMI | National Alliance on Mental Illness educational materials |
| MHA | Mental Health America resources |

### Tier 4 — Clinical Guidelines & Reviews

| Source Type | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| DSM-5-TR | Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders |
| ICD-11 | International Classification of Diseases, 11th Revision |
| Cochrane | Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews |
| Clinical Guideline | NICE, VA/DoD, or other recognized clinical practice guidelines |

### Tier 5 — Educational / Reference (Maximum: 20% of citations)

| Source Type | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| Mayo Clinic | Mayo Clinic disease and condition pages |
| Textbook | Published clinical or academic textbooks |
| Reference | Established medical encyclopedias, dictionaries |

### Blocked Sources — Never Cite These

- Wikipedia
- Psychology Today (editorial, not peer-reviewed)
- WebMD, Healthline, Verywell Mind, Medical News Today (consumer health sites)
- Reddit, Quora, Medium, or any social media platform
- Blog posts, influencer content, or marketing materials

### Recency Rule

At least 70% of citations must be published within the last 10 years. Seminal/foundational papers (e.g., Beck's cognitive model, DSM criteria definitions) are exempt from this rule but should be noted as such.

## Article Templates

### 1. Condition Deep-Dive

For each of the 45 conditions in the Psychage symptom navigator.

**Required Sections:**
1. **Overview** — What the condition is, prevalence, who it affects
2. **Signs & Symptoms** — Observable and subjective symptoms, how they manifest
3. **Causes & Risk Factors** — Biological, psychological, environmental, genetic
4. **Diagnosis** — How clinicians diagnose it, relevant criteria (DSM-5-TR/ICD-11)
5. **Treatment Options** — Evidence-based treatments (therapy, medication, lifestyle)
6. **Living With [Condition]** — Daily management, coping strategies, prognosis
7. **When to Seek Help** — Warning signs, crisis indicators, where to go
8. **References** — Full citation list

**Requirements:** ≥12 citations | ≥2,000 words | Medical disclaimer required | Link to symptom navigator conditions

### 2. Treatment & Therapy Guide

For evidence-based treatments: CBT, DBT, EMDR, medication classes, etc.

**Required Sections:**
1. **What Is [Treatment]** — Definition and brief history
2. **How It Works** — Mechanisms of action, theoretical basis
3. **What to Expect** — Session structure, timeline, what a patient experiences
4. **Effectiveness** — Research evidence, effect sizes, comparison data
5. **Who It's For** — Conditions treated, contraindications, ideal candidates
6. **Finding a Provider** — How to access this treatment, qualifications to look for
7. **References** — Full citation list

**Requirements:** ≥10 citations | ≥1,500 words | Medical disclaimer required

### 3. Research Digest

Breaking down recent studies into plain language.

**Required Sections:**
1. **Key Finding** — The headline result in one paragraph
2. **Study Details** — Who was studied, methodology, sample size, duration
3. **What This Means** — Practical implications for patients and clinicians
4. **Limitations** — What the study cannot tell us
5. **What's Next** — Future research directions, replication needs
6. **References** — Primary study + contextual citations

**Requirements:** ≥5 citations | ≥800 words | Must include link to original paper

### 4. Self-Help & Coping

Actionable, evidence-backed strategies.

**Required Sections:**
1. **Understanding the Challenge** — Context for why this matters
2. **Evidence-Based Strategies** — Numbered list of strategies, each citing research
3. **How to Get Started** — Practical first steps
4. **When Self-Help Isn't Enough** — Recognizing when professional help is needed
5. **References** — Full citation list

**Requirements:** ≥8 citations | ≥1,200 words | Each strategy must cite ≥1 study

### 5. Crisis & Safety

Urgent mental health content.

**Required Sections:**
1. **Recognizing the Signs** — Warning signs that a crisis is occurring
2. **Immediate Steps** — What to do right now
3. **Resources** — Hotlines, text lines, crisis centers with numbers
4. **Supporting Someone** — How to help a person in crisis
5. **Long-Term Safety Planning** — Creating a safety plan for ongoing risk
6. **References** — Full citation list

**Requirements:** ≥6 citations | ≥1,000 words | Crisis disclaimer at TOP | Mandatory clinical_admin review | Must link to all relevant crisis resources in navigator

## Language Guidelines

### Person-First Language (Default)

Write about people, not diagnoses.

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "a schizophrenic" | "a person with schizophrenia" |
| "the mentally ill" | "people with mental illness" |
| "an anorexic" | "a person with anorexia" |
| "substance abuser" / "drug addict" | "person with substance use disorder" |
| "the disabled" | "people with disabilities" |

**Exception:** Some communities prefer identity-first language (e.g., "autistic person" in the autistic community, "Deaf person" in the Deaf community). When writing about these communities, follow their stated preference and note it.

### Suicide Language

| Never Write | Write Instead |
|-------------|---------------|
| "committed suicide" | "died by suicide" |
| "successful suicide" | "death by suicide" |
| "failed suicide attempt" | "survived a suicide attempt" |

### Avoid Suffering/Victimhood Framing

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "suffers from depression" | "lives with depression" / "is diagnosed with depression" |
| "victim of trauma" | "person affected by trauma" / "trauma survivor" |
| "afflicted with" / "stricken with" | "diagnosed with" |
| "plagued by anxiety" | "experiences anxiety" |

### Avoid Minimizing Language

| Never Write | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| "just a phase" | Dismisses lived experience |
| "attention seeking" | Use "expressing a need for support" |
| "everyone gets sad sometimes" | Minimizes clinical depression |

### Clinical Terms as Adjectives

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "She is bipolar" | "She has bipolar disorder" |
| "He is OCD" | "He has OCD" |
| "They are ADHD" | "They have ADHD" |

### Strength-Based Framing

Focus on agency and resilience:
- "recovers" not "is cured"
- "manages symptoms" not "battles illness"
- "learns coping strategies" not "fights against"
- "seeks treatment" not "gets help for their problem"

## Readability Standards

**Target:** Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level ≤ 8 (6th–8th grade reading level)

### How to Achieve This

1. **Use short sentences** — aim for 15–20 words per sentence
2. **Use common words** — "use" not "utilize", "help" not "facilitate", "show" not "demonstrate"
3. **Define clinical terms** on first use — "Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is a type of talk therapy that..."
4. **Use active voice** — "CBT treats depression" not "Depression is treated by CBT"
5. **One idea per paragraph** — break complex concepts into digestible pieces
6. **Use headers and lists** — they improve scannability and comprehension
7. **Avoid acronyms** — spell out on first use, minimize repeated use

### Clinical Terms That Need Definition

Always define on first use: psychotherapy, cognitive-behavioral, pharmacotherapy, comorbidity, etiology, epidemiology, prevalence, incidence, efficacy, remission, relapse, neurotransmitter, serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine.

## Citation Format

Use APA 7th Edition style in the references section.

### Journal Article
> Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Issue), Pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx

### Government/Institutional Source
> Organization Name. (Year). *Title of document*. https://url

### Book
> Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of book* (Edition). Publisher.

### In-Text References

Use numbered superscript citations in body text that correspond to the References section:

> Cognitive-behavioral therapy demonstrates efficacy for generalized anxiety disorder, with meta-analyses showing response rates of 45–65%.^[1]

## Cross-Referencing

### Linking to Symptom Navigator

Every Condition Deep-Dive article must link to the corresponding condition(s) in the symptom navigator using `linked_condition_ids`. This enables:
- Users navigating from the navigator to educational content
- Contextual article suggestions after completing a symptom check

### Linking to Crisis Resources

Any article mentioning suicide, self-harm, or crisis situations must link to the relevant crisis resources in the navigator (29 resources available). Crisis & Safety articles must link to ALL relevant resources.

### Related Articles

Every article should reference 2–4 related articles to encourage deeper learning. Prioritize:
1. Articles covering related conditions (e.g., anxiety → depression)
2. Treatment articles for the condition being discussed
3. Self-help articles with practical strategies

## Medical Disclaimer

Required for Condition Deep-Dive, Treatment Guide, and Crisis & Safety articles.

Standard text:

> This article is for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of a qualified healthcare provider with any questions you may have regarding a mental health condition. If you are in crisis, call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or go to your nearest emergency room.

Place at the **top** of Crisis & Safety articles. Place at the **bottom** of other article types.

## Review Process

1. **Author** writes article following template and guidelines
2. **Quality Gate** runs automated checks (word count, citations, readability, sensitivity)
3. **Reviewer** (assigned via admin panel) evaluates 4 dimensions:
   - Clinical Accuracy (30% weight)
   - Readability (25% weight)
   - Completeness (25% weight)
   - Sensitivity (20% weight)
4. **Comments** with severity levels: Suggestion, Needs Fix, Critical
5. **Status flow:** Draft → In Review → Approved/Rejected → Published
6. Crisis & Safety articles require `clinical_admin` role for approval
